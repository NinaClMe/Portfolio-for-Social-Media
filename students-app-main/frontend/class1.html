Course overview and objectives

The course aims to equip you with the overall knowledge and skills to design, develop
and manage online media platforms, emphasizing key concepts, architecture, user behavior,
data analytics, ethical considerations and emerging technologies.


Definition of Designing and online medias

Online media, also known as digital media or digital content, refers to any type of content
or information that is created, distributed and consumed on the internet or through digital channels.
It includes websites, blogs, news articles, videos, podcasts, online magazines, social medias and more.


The evolution of online media platforms

  1. Early Internet Bulletin Boards and Forums (1990s)
  2. The Rise of Blogs and Personal Websites (Late 1990s - Early 2000s)
  3. Online News and Publications (Early 2000s)
  4. Social Media Platforms (Mid-2000s)
  5. User-Generated Content and Crowdsourcing (Late 2000s)
  6. Video Streaming and On-Demand Services (2010s)
  7. Live Streaming and Real-Time Communication (2010s)
  8. E-commerce and Social Commerce (2010s - Present)
  9. Podcasting (2010s - Present)
  10. Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR) (2010s - Present)
  11. AI and Personalization (2010s - Present)
  12. Niche and Micro-Networks (Present)

Terminology related to platform development using Instagram as an example

  • User interface and experience design:
    Always adjusting to new trends.
    Today: Stories, Reels and explore-page are very perosnalised because of the algorithm.

  • Features and functionalities:
    Like, comment, DMs (Direct messages), tagging, posting, Live-Streams, donations,
    selling options for business (shop), following people, analytics, verifications (blue hook)
    polls, close friend stories, broadcast-channels

  • Target audience and user demographics:
    At the beginning it was younger people who were targeted and now it's everyone.

  • Monetization strategies:
    Influencer, Advertisement (personal, brands or organizations), verifications (paying for the blue hook)

  • Privacy and security measures:
    The positive things are report, block, no comments (on posts or stories), no sharing of stories, privat account,
    2-Factor-Authentication, communitiy guidelines The negative things are Bots, hacking, data privacy Meta

  • Recent updates and innovations:
    Shared Posts, notes, threads, broadcast-channels, paid "premium" content
